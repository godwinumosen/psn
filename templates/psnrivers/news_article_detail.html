The Executive Council of the Pharmaceutical Society of Nigeria, Rivers State Branch, has released comprehensive updated guidelines for community pharmacy practice. These guidelines are designed to align local practices with national standards and international best practices.

The new guidelines address several key areas:

  1. Patient Counseling Standards
    All community pharmacies are now required to provide dedicated counseling areas where patients can discuss their medications in private. Pharmacists must ensure adequate time is allocated for patient education, particularly for new medications or complex regimens.
  2. Medication Safety Protocols
    Enhanced protocols for medication dispensing have been introduced, including mandatory double-checking systems for high-risk medications and improved labeling requirements.
  3. Documentation Requirements
    Community pharmacies must maintain comprehensive records of dispensed medications, patient counseling sessions, and any adverse drug reactions reported by patients.
  4. Continuing Professional Development
    Pharmacists are required to complete a minimum of 40 CPD hours annually, with at least 10 hours focused on patient safety and medication management.
  5. Premises Standards
    Updated requirements for pharmacy premises include improved storage conditions for medications, adequate lighting, and proper waste disposal systems for expired or damaged medications.

The guidelines take effect from January 1, 2025, with a six-month transition period for full compliance. The PSN Rivers will conduct orientation sessions for community pharmacists to ensure smooth implementation.
